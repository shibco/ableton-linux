# Code of Conduct

Para-Real Ltd.

## PREAMBLE

This repository is produced by Para-Real Ltd. This Code of Conduct provides clarity on how we operate it and the standards we hold ourselves and everyone who participates in it to. The project is independent and unofficial; it is not endorsed by or affiliated with Ableton.

The people who participate in this repository are a diverse cohort that asserts that *another future is possible*. They come from a variety of cultural, ethnic and professional backgrounds; the work and the issues we grapple with together are often provocative, emotional and urgent. We are dedicated to building an inclusive and harassment-free environment in this repository for everyone regardless of gender, gender identity and expression, race, ethnicity, sexual orientation, disability, age, religion (or lack thereof), body size, physical appearance, social class or language ability (ability to speak English).

We aspire to live by our values and behave in ways that positively impact our communities, how we treat each other, how we interact with one another, and how we see the world. This Code operationalises our para-real ethos: sovereignty, mutual care, zero extraction. Any behaviour that erodes these principles will be excluded.

This Code covers *everybody* when they participate in this repository, including but not limited to:

- the core Para-Real Ltd. team working on the repository;
- maintainers, contributors and reviewers;
- people who open or comment on issues, pull requests or commits;
- testers who publish results or diagnostic material in the repository; and
- members of the public who otherwise interact with the repository.

This Code does not cover any Discord server, the wider Ableton or Linux communities, Para-Real Ltd.'s other spaces, events, external support channels or private activity outside this repository.

We encourage a collaborative – non-competitive – culture. When it comes to criticism, disagreement, and debate, we expect better and hope you do, too. It is not enough to feel antagonised or frustrated when engaging with others. We ask that you guide instead of punish and collectively give space for growth and personal evolution.

At the same time, this repository is dedicated to inclusivity and sensitivity. We exclude harassment, toxicity, predatory or discriminatory behaviour from it. We reserve swift, decisive action when safety is at stake.

When you interact or engage with this repository in any capacity, you agree to:

### Practice tolerance and inclusion.

We will not tolerate homophobic, transphobic, racist, ableist and sexist slurs and content, even if intended as a joke, or as an ironic remark. That also includes demeaning, belittling or otherwise verbally intimidating communication. In short, any attitudes that may promote the oppression and/or exclusion of historically marginalised groups will not be accepted.

### Engage honestly.

We talk to and with people rather than about people and/or groups. That means no sub-posting, unwelcome, or otherwise toxic comments regarding a person’s lifestyle choices and practices, antagonising and incendiary generalisations, flaming or edgy remarks at the expense of someone or another community. While we thrive on sarcasm, critique and the need to seek support and alliances while expressing defeating feelings, we ask that you avoid persistent trauma dumping. This repository should not stand in as your personal punching bag.

### Be sensitive to others.

Different people perceive the world in different ways, and we expect everyone participating in this repository to be sensitive to how their actions are interpreted. We have a zero tolerance policy to harassment. Our definition of harassment includes: offensive verbal comments related to – including but not limited to – gender, gender identity and expression, race, ethnicity, sexual orientation, disability, age, religion (or lack thereof), body size, physical appearance, social class or language ability (ability to speak English), deliberate physical or written intimidation, stalking, harassing photography or recording, sustained disruption of repository discussions, inappropriate contact, and unwelcome sexual attention.

### Be careful of people's privacy.

Whether working with diagnostic data, test reports, logs, or simply participating in this repository, think before you share. Do not access user data you're not entitled to or disclose information someone provided in confidence. Do not post photos of others online without their consent. Do not share personally identifying information posted by repository participants to other places. Do not post the personal details of others, do not engage in doxing, and do not re-post or re-share links to sites or resources that share or weaponise personal details without first checking with the Para-Real Ltd. team.

Review diagnostic output before publishing it. Do not post Ableton installers, authorisation files, licence keys, projects, samples, recordings, account details or plug-in credentials.

### De-escalate conflict.

This repository values fierce debate and is made up of people who hold opposing views. Disagreements, both social and technical, happen all the time, and we expect participants to resolve disagreements and de-escalate. Refusing to disengage, trolling maliciously or sealioning, will not be tolerated.

### Keep the community safe.

Hate speech, such as, but not limited to: white supremacy, ethnostate advocacy, discussion of national socialism / Nazism will not be tolerated, and will be ejected without further notice. When engaging with such content in the wider world, use discretion and consider the potential effects of signal boosting this kind of content into the repository.

### Communicate thoughtfully.

We encourage people to share their work, interests, related projects, relevant calls and upcoming events when they contribute to the work of the repository. At the same time, this repository is not an advertising timeline / hustlespace. If you wish to promote something, please engage with the repository beyond self interest.

### Respect our work.

Materials in this repository are licensed as stated in the [LICENCE](LICENCE) file and any component-specific notices unless explicitly stated otherwise. We encourage people to share our work, through links, citation and curation, and are open to remix or extension under those licences. Ableton Live itself is proprietary and is not included in this repository. Users must supply their own copy and valid licence. Do not use the repository to share Ableton installers, authorisation bypasses, licence files or credentials.

### Keep our spaces clear of crime and harm.

You are responsible for the legal risks you take in your own jurisdiction. Do not use this repository to distribute or coordinate things that are clearly criminal or harmful in most places: for example malware, pirated software at scale, sexualised depictions of children (including cartoons or AI-generated imagery), doxxing, explicit incitement to violence, or vandalism and similar acts carried out “for the bit”. We will not host material that uses the repository as cover for abuse or that would reasonably force us into direct legal conflict. Users who treat the repository as a channel for this kind of activity will be removed. This rule is about clear harms and obvious abuse of our infrastructure; it is not a ban on political disagreement, criticism of institutions, or discussion of identity.

### Respect the security of our infrastructure and community.

Our systems exist to serve collective development, testing and dialogue, not as attack surfaces or data mines. Do not target repository participants or the Para-Real Ltd. team with malware, social engineering or other digital security attacks. Do not engage in “just-curious” vulnerability hunting against the repository or its release infrastructure without written permission from the core team. If you discover a flaw, tell us at [security@parare.al](mailto:security@parare.al). We practice responsible disclosure and will credit you where appropriate.

### Refrain from propaganda ops.

This repository is not a staging ground for influence campaigns. Do not post content on behalf of a government, corporation, or political actor without full disclosure, orchestrate multiple accounts to manufacture consensus, harass, or derail discussion, drop manipulated or selectively edited material to steer opinion under false pretenses, or otherwise use the repository to funnel participants into external ideological or commercial movements. If your work intersects with state, corporate, or activist comms, declare the affiliation up front. Transparency keeps our critical work effective; subterfuge and psyops are not welcome.

### Defend the community from networked abuse.

Co-ordinated raids, synthetic impersonation, disinformation seeding, or dataset scraping are designed to overwhelm dialogue and harvest trust. Any such attack results in instant ejection and, where necessary, public disclosure of the incident so allies can harden their own defenses.

## RAISING A BREACH OF CONDUCT

If you believe someone has breached the Code of Conduct while participating in this repository, we ask that you inform us.

- Signal: Send a secure message or voice memo to +1 413 679 0560
- Email: [coc@parare.al](mailto:coc@parare.al)

Our team operates primarily on Central European Time.

**Your correspondence will be kept confidential.** We deal with all issues in private and in consultation with the affected party. If the breach is severe, or has implications for repository participants at large, we may be required to discuss it publicly. We will look to the affected party for guidance.

**If you believe anyone is in physical danger, please notify appropriate authorities or emergency services first.** If you are unsure who to contact, please share this uncertainty and we will offer assistance where possible.

When contacting us about a Code of Conduct breach, please include as much information as you can so that we can respond with care and attention:

- Your preferred contact info.
- Names or usernames of any individuals involved. If there were other witnesses besides you, please try to include them as well.
- When and where in the repository the incident occurred. Please be as specific as possible.
- Your account of what occurred. If there is a publicly available record (e.g. an issue, pull request, review or commit exchange) please include a link or screenshots.
- Any extra context you believe existed for the incident.
- If you believe this incident is ongoing.
- Any other information you believe we should have to adequately respond.

If you are unsure whether your concern would be considered a breach, or whether the conduct happened within the repository covered by this Code of Conduct, we encourage you to still raise it with us.

## RESPONDING TO A BREACH

| TYPE | BREACHES | ACTION | REVIEW |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 'RED-LIST' BREACHES | Hate or extremist content, doxxing & privacy attacks, credible threats, sexual exploitation, malware distribution, deepfakes/synthetic impersonation, coordinated raids, dataset scraping, disinformation seeding, covert propaganda ops | Immediate restriction / ejection. No prior warning. | Internal review within 48 hours to confirm or extend the sanction. |
| EVERYTHING ELSE | Personal attacks, persistent disruption, privacy lapses, unapproved advertising, etc. | First: warning, second: temporary restriction, third: ejection. | Resolution target: 7 days. |

We confer with those targeted to choose the least harmful remedy, including conflict resolution, de-escalation, or, where desired, facilitated reconciliation. Money is never a shield: financial support or commercial standing does not exempt anyone from enforcement.

Severe cases (e.g. criminal content, credible threats of violence) may be referred to relevant external parties or authorities.

All enforcement decisions rest with the Para-Real Ltd. team and are final.
